## Executive summary

AKT1 (HGNC:391; Entrez 207; chromosome 14q32) encodes RAC-alpha serine/threonine-protein kinase, a central effector of the PI3K/AKT signaling axis that governs cell survival, proliferation, glucose metabolism, and apoptosis suppression across virtually all tissues — Bgee records 273 present calls, consistent with ubiquitous expression. The protein carries a pleckstrin homology domain for membrane recruitment and a kinase domain, supported by 42 experimental PDB crystal structures and an AlphaFold model with a global pLDDT of 83.62. Clinically, a single ClinVar pathogenic variant (p.Glu17Lys; c.49G>A) drives constitutive activation and underlies Proteus syndrome and Cowden-like overgrowth, while AlphaMissense flags 100+ additional missense changes as likely pathogenic, concentrated around residues F438–F472 and the activation-loop site S473. As a drug target, capivasertib (AZD5363) has reached Phase 4 approval, with MK-2206 and miransertib in Phase 2, and PIK3CA mutation, PTEN loss, and AKT1 somatic mutation are the key predictive biomarkers for AKT pathway inhibitor response.
