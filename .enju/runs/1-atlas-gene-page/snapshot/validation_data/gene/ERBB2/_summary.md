## Executive summary

ERBB2 (HER2; HGNC:3430) encodes a receptor tyrosine kinase located at chromosome 17q12 and is one of the most clinically important oncogenes in human cancer, serving as the primary driver and therapeutic target in HER2-positive breast, gastric, and non-small cell lung cancers. The gene spans ~42.5 kb on the forward strand (GRCh38: 39,687,914–39,730,426), produces 34 Ensembl transcripts, and its canonical protein (UniProt P04626) carries a tyrosine kinase domain that transduces growth and survival signals through MAPK, PI3K/AKT, and ErbB heterodimer pathways. Clinically, roughly 400 ClinVar variants have been catalogued, with pathogenic variants concentrated in the kinase domain; AlphaMissense scores 8,156 missense positions, with 100+ classified as likely pathogenic. More than 100 molecules target ERBB2 in ChEMBL, including FDA-approved antibodies (trastuzumab, pertuzumab) and small-molecule TKIs (lapatinib, tucatinib, afatinib), with trastuzumab alone spanning 864+ clinical trials. Expression is near-ubiquitous across 276 of 297 tissue conditions, with highest scores in epithelial and nervous tissues.
