### Pharmacogenomics & Clinical Considerations

**Key drug-gene interactions:**
- **HER2 expression/amplification:** Predictive biomarker for all HER2-directed therapies; FISH, IHC, or NGS required for patient selection
- **PIK3CA mutations:** Associated with reduced trastuzumab benefit; activating mutations may predict dual HER2/PI3K pathway inhibition benefit
- **TP53 mutations:** Possible association with improved response to neoadjuvant trastuzumab-containing regimens
- **PGx genes affecting metabolism:** Limited; most HER2-targeted agents have broad tolerability; no formal dosing guidelines based on polymorphisms

**Approved therapeutic indications:**
- HER2-positive breast cancer (early-stage and metastatic)
- HER2-positive gastric and gastroesophageal junction cancer
- HER2-positive non-small cell lung cancer (select agents)

**Notable dosing/management:**
- Trastuzumab: Loading dose 4 mg/kg IV, then 2 mg/kg weekly or 6 mg/kg every 3 weeks; subcutaneous formulation available (600 mg fixed dose)
- Lapatinib: 1250 mg once daily (with food) or 1500 mg daily in combination regimens
- Afatinib: 40 mg daily (HER2+, dose-reduced to 20 mg for toxicity)
- Pertuzumab: 840 mg loading dose, then 420 mg every 3 weeks
- Tucatinib: 300 mg twice daily (with food)

**Cardiotoxicity monitoring:** Trastuzumab and other HER2 inhibitors require baseline and periodic LVEF assessment (echocardiography/MUGA) due to risk of cardiomyopathy and heart failure.
